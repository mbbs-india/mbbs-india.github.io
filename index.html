Kyrgyz State Medical Academy

I.K. Akhunbaev Kyrgyz State Medical Academy (KSMA) is a leading higher medical institution of the Kyrgyz Republic with more than 75 years of history. All these years the flagship of medical education in Kyrgyzstan underwent a glorious path of development. Today, our Academy is one of the leading medical universities in Central Asia, having perfect reputation in the training of highly qualified doctors. We are well known in many countries of the world through scientific and practical achievements of our graduates working in almost all corners of the globe. The main purpose of the training of specialists is implementation of practical health care through training that is focused on providing of quality services by general practitioners and promotion of them in regions. The most important requirement of training of general practitioners is mostly on an outpatient basis, allowing mastering the basic principles of general practice, such as versatility, continuous monitoring and preventive activities. There are 600 highly qualified faculty members, including 7 Academicians of National Academy of Sciences of the Kyrgyz Republic (NAS KR), 4 Corresponding Members of NAS KR, 55 Doctors of Sciences and 215 PhD (Candidates of Sciences) at KSMA. Currently, there are more than 6000 students from Kyrgyzstan and 19 foreign countries. Education is conducted in Kyrgyz, Russian and English.

Entry exams:

  • Foreign citizens who enter to the first year of study at the “Faculty for foreign citizens” take oral entry exams (in English) in the following subjects: Biology, Chemistry and English
  • Foreign citizens with good Russian and who has graduated Russian language courses enter to the faculties with Russian language studies and take entry exams in Chemistry, Biology and Russian

PRE-UNIVERSITY TRAINING

Preparatory Department for Foreign Citizens of I.K. Akhunbaev Kyrgyz State Medical Academy offers training on the contractual basis in the following subjects: Russian, Chemistry and Biology for further admission in KSMA and other higher educational institutions of the Kyrgyz Republic. The duration of study is 9 months. For entering to Preparatory Department for Foreign Citizens have to be submitted:

  • Original document of secondary education, equivalent to the state document on the general intermediate (complete) degree awarded in the Kyrgyz Republic
  • Translation of original document with notification
  • Passport with appropriate entry visa
  • Medical certificate of health status, form 086
  • 10 photos, sized 3x4 cm.

Admission

How to apply

The attraction and admission of foreign prospective students is conducted in cooperation with official representatives having official agreements with ISM and relevant authorities.

Agreements between the ISM and official representatives include the following liabilities: promote public relations in home country, check authenticity of students’ documents, and guarantee that applicants have no other aims except for the aim to receive medical education while staying in the Kyrgyz Republic.

Arrival and training expenses of foreign students at ISM include: admission fee, tuition fee, hostel accommodation fee. These payments must be made according to ISM rules and procedures. Living expenses of foreign students in Kyrgyz Republic including food, extension of visa and registration fee as well as other expenses must be covered by students on their own in compliance with ISM rules and procedures.

The Department of Pediatrics, Obstetrics and Gynecology was established in September 2008 on the basis of Department of special clinical disciplines.

Head of chair is MD, Professor Alekseev Vladimir Petrovich.

The chair includes the following curricular disciplines:

  • Introduction to Pediatrics,
  • Infant diseases, or Pediatrics,
  • Course of  Acute and Chronic Child diseases
  • Obstetrics,
  • Gynecology,
  • Infant infectious diseases

 The aim of studying Pediatrics is to promote:

  • Formation of communication skills that will facilitate the clinical interaction with children, adolescents and their families.
  • Development of competency in the physical examination of infants, children and adolescents.
  • Acquisition of the knowledge necessary for the diagnosis and initial management of common children illnesses.
  • Ø Advance of clinical problem-solving skills.
  • Ø An understanding of the influence of family, community and society on the child in health and disease.
  • Ø Comprehension of strategies for health promotion as well as diseases and injury prevention.
  • Ø Development of the attitudes and professional behavior appropriate for clinical practice.

 The care of the child and the treatment of his illness essentially differ from the approaches used in medicine for adults. For the child there are characteristic anatomic, physiological, morphological, emotional and immunological immaturity which defines feature of current and an outcome of disease. The child organism constantly grows and develops. As growing child’s reaction to surrounding, his food needs, ability to resist to infections and to take various diseases essentially changes. 

Hence, the correct estimation of the laboratory tests and understanding of an originality of children pathology are possible according to deep knowledge of anatomical-physiological features of a growing organism of the child. There is necessary the knowledge of laws of children’s physical, psychological development for the correct organization of day regimen, care and feeding and diseases prevention.  

 Obstetrics and Gynecology forms the one of the main sections of general medicine. 

The aim of studying Obstetrics and Gynecology is to promote

  • Study the main methods of examinations of the pregnant women, women who recently confined newborns and gynecological patients.
  • Use practical knowledge for conduction clinical analysis of symptoms of pathological conditions during pregnancy, delivery process and gynecological diseases;
  • Gain experience in statement of the diagnosis and treatment.

 Department has its own lecture halls. Practical lessons are conducted in the leading clinics of Bishkek as National Center of Maternal and Childhood Welfare, Bishkek City’s Children Clinical Hospital and Ambulance, Clinical maternity hospital No 3, 4, 6, and Republic Clinical Infection Hospital.

The Department of Scientific Disciplines was reorganized on the basis of Chair of Scientific Disciplines and Public Health in 2009. It is located on the base of Institute of Mountain Physiology of the KR National academy of Sciences.

Teaching activities include clinical and theory classes on following disciplines: General chemistry, Pharmacology, Medical physics and High Mathematics, Histology, and Computer science.

Brief content of disciplines

Chemistry: The study of exchange of matter and energy in living organism on the base of laws of  chemical thermodynamics, kinetics and chemical equilibrium. Electrochemistry. Electrode potentials. Biogenic elements. Colloid state of substance. Disperse systems. Organic compounds. Biopolimers and their structural components.

Histology: Cytology. General embryology. Epithelial tissue. Glands. Blood and lymph. Hemopoiesis. Proper connective tissue. Skeletal connective tissue. Muscle tissue. Nervous tissue. Cardiovascular system. Endocrine system. Digestive system. Respiratory system. Urogenital system. Skeleto-muscular system. Nervous system. Reproduction. Human embryology.

Pharmacology: Pharmacology basis. Affecting on autonomic and peripheral nervous system means. Affecting on central nervous system means. Chemiotherapeutic means. Hormonal preparations, their synthetical analogs and antagonists. Anticancerous means. Immunomodulaters.

Medical physics and high mathematics. Mathematical methods in medicine. Statistical set. Mathematical modeling of systems. Biomechanics. Concept of biomechanics. Membranes biophysics. Biophysics of nervouus impulse.  Hemodynamics. X-rays. Ultrasonic investigation.

American University of Central Asia

Founded in 1993, AUCA develops future leaders for the democratic transformation of Central Asia. American University of Central Asia is an international, multi-disciplinary learning community in the American liberal arts tradition. Accomodation

The AUCA International Dormitory is a gathering place for the exchange of ideas and cultures. It also offers a safe and secure place for students to live and study. The AUCA International Dormitory welcomes up to 140 students each year and provides all needed facilities for students’ residence. Dormitory administration practice Cultural Mix policy, which means that residents are placed together with representatives of different cultures and nations in order to initiate the development of language skills and the integration of international students to AUCA and Kyrgyzstani communities.

All semester-long exchange students are required to live in the Dormitory. Year-long exchange students may opt out of the Dormitory in their second semester in favor of an apartment or home stay. Full-time international students, especially freshmen and students of the Preparatory Program are encouraged to live in the Dormitory to help them adjust to life in Bishkek and the demands of an AUCA education. Scholarship-recepints whose housing fee is covered by Donors need to clarify terms of housing policy of their Grants with the Grants Office.

For more details visit AUCA website

General Requirements

  • HIGH-SCHOOL-DIPLOMA (Completed intermediate education(12 years), equivalent to secondary education of the KR)
  • HIGH SCHOOL TRANSCRIPT

AUCA Admission Requirements

  • AUCA APPLICATION
  • STATEMENT OF PURPOSE
  • ADMISSIONS EXAM SCORES
  • PASSPORT COPY AND PHOTOS
  • STUDENT VISA APPLICATION

KSMA Admission Requirements

  • The reference from Ministry of Education and Science of the Kyrgyz Republic on equivalency of full secondary and intermediate education
  • The passport with the corresponding entry visa
  • 10 photos, the size 3x4 cm
  • Have no serious health problems, which has to be officially confirmed by medical references “for higher educational institution”
  • The certificate on studies of Russian language for those who wants to get admission in faculties with Russian language of studies

IMI Admission Requirements

Undergraduate Applicants pre-requisites:

  • 50% rating scores in PCB
  • Have no serious health problems, which has to be officially confirmed by medical references “for higher educational institution”
  • Admission fee (wouldnt be returned on violations of DOC 1A)
  • Bank statement

Necessary documents (originals)

  • Reference of Ministry of Education of the KYRGYZ REPUBLIC on equivalency to secondary education of the KYRGYZ REPUBLIC
  • Passport with appropriate entry visa
  • Translation of original document with notification
  • Medical reference (medical inspection can be provided in the Medical Center of the IMI)
  • 8 photos (3×4)

Postgraduate Applicants:

  • Original diploma on higher medical education;
  • Copy of Passport
  • Filled Application form
  • Medical fitness certificate

ISM ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS

Necessary conditions:

  • 50 % rating scores
  • Admission fee (is not returned on violation)
  • Paying capacity

Necessary documents (originals):

  • Passport with appropriate entry visa
  • Medical reference – 086 form, (medical inspection can be provided in the Medical Center of the ISM)
  • Application form
  • 10 photographs (3х4cm)

Necessary documents when being transferred from other Higher Medical Institution:

  • Academic reference from previous Higher Medical Institution
  • Extract from order on expulsion from previous Higher Medical Institution
  • Certificate of Preparatory department
  • Passport with appropriate entry visa
  • Medical reference – 086 form, (medical inspection can be provided in the Medical Center of the ISM)
  • Application form
  • 10 photographs (3х4)
